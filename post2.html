The SoULian way of INDIA's 70th year of independence

You must be the change you want to see in the World.

Article by Divya Kaundinya

The atmosphere filled with nationalism ,where every citizen of India ,although culturally diverse ,integrated and moved in a unison , became secular ,to celebrate the 70th independence day ,where the TRICOLOUR waved,and the National Anthem ROARED. Unchaining all the shackles of communal violence and gender discrimination, we truly became a SOCIALIST DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC. Amidst the thunderous ROAR of the national anthem, among the citizens of India ,we a team of highly motivated 'SoULs', dressed in the SoUL attire which read "Empowering lives, enlightening minds', "The round pegs in square holes" made a very small yet effective contribution. Motivated by the idea of 'Swachh Bharath Abhiyaan' a govt of India initiative ,and the spreading DENGUE, we took an initiative to perform a street play to promote cleanliness and hygiene both in private and public. INSPIRED by Dr. Kalam's msg which says" to unify India and to empower her ,we must empower the rural areas and integrate them in the nationalistic idea" we also enacted a play in DODDAKONDGOLLA about the DENGUE and promoted ' Swachh Bharath Abhiyaan '. We were heartily welcomed by the residents, who sat patiently and appreciated our efforts as we performed in front of them. We also thank the people of hassan for supporting the play we also extend our gratitude to the rickshawala's of simhasanapuri autostand for making way for us and supporting us in all possible ways so that we could perform without any hassle. The response of the audience in Hassan and the appreciation from a senior citizen who was a part of the freedom struggle was what kept us going. Best wishes and donations that came in to our organization from the audience's end brought up our energy level. Our happiness knew no bounds when the people recognized our efforts and appreciated our organization that we are all proud to be a part of. This was our vision for this year's Independence Day, what's yours?
